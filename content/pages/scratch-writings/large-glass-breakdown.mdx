## Geometric Definitions

_Source: Roberto Giunti, "The Bachelor Stripped Bare by Cabri Géomètre, Even" (2007), citing Duchamp's notes._

### Bachelor Apparatus (General Forms)

<Quote>
  The principal forms of the bachelor apparatus or utensil are imperfect: Rectangle, circle, square,
  parallelepiped, symmetrical handle; demisphere.
</Quote>

### The Chocolate Grinder

<Quote>The threads could also be related to the geometric concept of ruled surface.</Quote>

<Quote>The rollers do not interpenetrate as the device is grinding.</Quote>

### The Water Mill

<Quote>
  The wheels will rotate forward and backward, the paddles will be over and under, in front and
  back.
</Quote>

<Quote>
  The rotation of the water wheels was intended to enable the onanistic left-right motion of the
  Chariot.
</Quote>

### The Sieves / Parasols

<Quote>
  The spangles pass through the parasols A, C, D, E, F…B. and as they gradually arrive at D, E, F, …
  etc. they are straightened out.
</Quote>

<Quote>The group of these parasols forms a sort of labyrinth of the three directions.</Quote>

### The Chariot / Glider

<Quote>The Chariot will turn inside out as if it were a glove.</Quote>

### The Bride's Realm (Upper Half)

<Quote>
  In the Bride – the principal forms will be more or less large or small, no longer have
  mensurability in relation to their destination.
</Quote>

<Quote>
  Parabolas, hyperbolas (or volumes deriving from them) will lose all connotation of men-surated
  position.
</Quote>

### The Fourth Dimension Formula

<Quote>**Perspective + Transparency + Motion = Emulation of 4D spatiality**</Quote>

<Quote>One must consult the book, and see the two together — to remove the retinal aspect.</Quote>
